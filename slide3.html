The Problem with Traditional ML-based IDS

They Learn Correlations, Not Causation

Standard ML models are excellent at finding statistical patterns, but they don't understand the underlying cause-and-effect dynamics of a network.

Benign "Flash Crowd"

Many legitimate users access a server at once.

High Traffic High CPU Load

Malicious DDoS Attack

A botnet floods a server with malicious requests.

High Traffic High CPU Load

The Consequence: High False Positives

A correlational model sees the same pattern (High Traffic → High CPU) and may misclassify the benign event as an attack, leading to alert fatigue.
